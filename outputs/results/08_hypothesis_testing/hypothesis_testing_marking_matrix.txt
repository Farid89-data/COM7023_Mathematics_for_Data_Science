STEP 2: HYPOTHESIS TESTING FRAMEWORK
======================================================================

--- The Scientific Method ---

  1. State the null hypothesis (H₀) and alternative hypothesis (H₁)
  2. Choose significance level (α)
  3. Collect data and calculate test statistic
  4. Compute p-value or compare to critical value
  5. Make decision: reject or fail to reject H₀

--- Key Definitions ---

  Null Hypothesis (H₀): Statement of 'no effect' or 'no difference'
  Alternative Hypothesis (H₁): Statement we want to provide evidence for
  Significance Level (α): Probability of Type I error (usually 0.05)
  P-value: Probability of observing data as extreme or more, given H₀ is true

--- Decision Rule ---

  If p-value < α: Reject H₀ (statistically significant)
  If p-value ≥ α: Fail to reject H₀ (not statistically significant)

--- Types of Errors ---

  Type I Error (False Positive): Reject H₀ when H₀ is true
    Probability = α

  Type II Error (False Negative): Fail to reject H₀ when H₀ is false
    Probability = β
    Power = 1 - β (probability of correctly rejecting false H₀)

STEP 3: ONE-SAMPLE T-TEST
======================================================================

--- When to Use ---
  - Testing if a sample mean differs from a known value
  - Population standard deviation is unknown
  - Data is approximately normally distributed

--- Test Statistic ---

           x̄ - μ₀
    t = ─────────────
          s / √n

  Degrees of freedom: df = n - 1

--- Example: Testing if High Grade Band Descriptions Average 30 Words ---

  H₀: μ = 30 (mean word count is 30)
  H₁: μ ≠ 30 (mean word count is not 30)
  α = 0.05 (two-tailed test)

--- Calculation ---
  Sample size: n = 8
  Sample mean: x̄ = 29.3750
  Sample std dev: s = 17.5168
  Hypothesized mean: μ₀ = 30

  t = (29.3750 - 30) / (17.5168 / √8)
  t = -0.6250 / 6.1931
  t = -0.1009

  Degrees of freedom: df = 7
  P-value (two-tailed) = 0.922445

  Critical value (α/2 = 0.025): t_crit = ±2.3646

--- Verification (scipy.stats.ttest_1samp) ---
  t-statistic = -0.1009
  p-value = 0.922445

--- Decision (α = 0.05) ---
  P-value (0.922445) ≥ α (0.05)
  Fail to reject H₀: Insufficient evidence mean differs from 30

STEP 4: TWO-SAMPLE T-TEST (INDEPENDENT SAMPLES)
======================================================================

--- When to Use ---
  - Comparing means of two independent groups
  - Testing if there's a significant difference between groups

--- Test Statistic (Equal Variances) ---

           x̄₁ - x̄₂
    t = ──────────────────────
        sp × √(1/n₁ + 1/n₂)

  where sp is the pooled standard deviation

--- Example: Comparing High vs Low Grade Band Word Counts ---

  H₀: μ_high = μ_low (no difference in mean word counts)
  H₁: μ_high ≠ μ_low (difference exists)
  α = 0.05

--- Sample Statistics ---
  High Grade Bands (90-100, 80-90):
    n₁ = 8
    x̄₁ = 29.3750
    s₁ = 17.5168

  Low Grade Bands (30-39, 20-29, 0-19):
    n₂ = 12
    x̄₂ = 38.7500
    s₂ = 14.6171

--- Levene's Test for Equal Variances ---
  F-statistic = 0.0026
  p-value = 0.9601
  Equal variances assumed: True

--- Two-Sample t-Test Results ---
  t-statistic = -1.2993
  p-value = 0.210234

--- Decision (α = 0.05) ---
  P-value (0.210234) ≥ α (0.05)
  Fail to reject H₀: No significant difference detected

--- Effect Size (Cohen's d) ---
  d = -0.5931
  Interpretation: Medium effect

STEP 5: PAIRED T-TEST
======================================================================

--- When to Use ---
  - Comparing two measurements on the same subjects
  - Before-after comparisons
  - Matched pairs design

--- Test Statistic ---

           d̄
    t = ─────────
        sd / √n

  where d̄ is the mean of differences

--- Example: Comparing Adjacent Grade Bands (90-100 vs 80-90) ---

  H₀: μ_d = 0 (no difference between adjacent grade descriptions)
  H₁: μ_d ≠ 0 (difference exists)

--- Difference Statistics ---
  Differences: [-12 -32 -49 -12]
  Mean difference (d̄) = -26.2500
  Std of differences (sd) = 17.8582
  Number of pairs (n) = 4

--- Calculation ---
  t = -26.2500 / (17.8582 / √4)
  t = -2.9398
  df = 3
  p-value = 0.060519

--- Verification (scipy.stats.ttest_rel) ---
  t-statistic = -2.9398
  p-value = 0.060519

STEP 6: CHI-SQUARE TEST
======================================================================

--- Chi-Square Goodness of Fit Test ---

  Tests if observed frequencies match expected frequencies

  Test Statistic:
              (Oᵢ - Eᵢ)²
    χ² = Σ  ─────────────
               Eᵢ

--- Example: Testing if Word Count Categories are Equally Distributed ---

  H₀: Word counts are equally distributed across categories
  H₁: Word counts are not equally distributed

--- Observed Frequencies ---
    Low: 6
    Medium: 14
    High: 16

--- Expected Frequencies (under H₀) ---
    Low: 12.00
    Medium: 12.00
    High: 12.00

--- Calculation ---
  χ² = Σ (Oᵢ - Eᵢ)² / Eᵢ
    Category 1: (6 - 12.00)² / 12.00 = 3.0000
    Category 2: (14 - 12.00)² / 12.00 = 0.3333
    Category 3: (16 - 12.00)² / 12.00 = 1.3333
  χ² = 4.6667
  df = 2
  p-value = 0.096972

--- Verification (scipy.stats.chisquare) ---
  χ² = 4.6667
  p-value = 0.096972

STEP 7: CONFIDENCE INTERVALS
======================================================================

--- Definition ---

  A confidence interval provides a range of plausible values for a
  population parameter. A 95% CI means: if we repeated the sampling
  many times, 95% of the intervals would contain the true parameter.

--- Formula for Mean (σ unknown) ---

            s
  x̄ ± t_α/2 × ───
            √n

--- 95% Confidence Interval for High Grade Word Counts ---

  Sample Statistics:
    n = 8
    x̄ = 29.3750
    s = 17.5168
    SE = s/√n = 6.1931

  Critical Value:
    t_0.025,7 = 2.3646

  Confidence Interval:
    CI = 29.3750 ± 2.3646 × 6.1931
    CI = 29.3750 ± 14.6444
    CI = [14.7306, 44.0194]

  Interpretation:
    We are 95% confident that the true mean word count for high
    grade bands is between 14.73 and 44.02

--- Confidence Intervals at Different Levels ---
     Level    t-critical      Margin                   Interval
  ------------------------------------------------------------
       90%        1.8946     11.7334  [17.6416, 41.1084]
       95%        2.3646     14.6444  [14.7306, 44.0194]

STEP 8: NON-PARAMETRIC TESTS
======================================================================

--- When to Use Non-Parametric Tests ---
  - Data is not normally distributed
  - Small sample sizes
  - Ordinal or ranked data
  - Outliers present
